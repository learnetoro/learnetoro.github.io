If so, eToro, a social trading and investment platform makes it a hell of a lot easier. So, what exactly is eToro? Well, eToro is a platform that allows the average person like you or me to trade stocks and shares like a pro! And the best thing about it is, you don’t need to know anything about trading to get started. If you’re wondering how you could possibly trade stocks and shares without specific skills or training, let me explain… The first cool feature of eToro is virtual trading. This feature allows you to sign up and use imaginary money to try out the site. That way you can check it out and see what kind success you have before you decide to invest any of your own cash. Another handy feature is the copy function. The copy function allows you to choose traders you like and copy their moves. This means that when you click copy, it will copy all of their transactions from that point on. Of course you can set it within your own budget. That way when they make 15% profit, so do you! Before deciding on who you’re going to copy, you can browse the portfolios of investors, view their transactions, their earnings and their trade history. It’s super easy. But let’s get into the less exciting end of things, taxes! We all have to pay them and that’s no different for virtual traders using platforms like eToro. So what kind of taxation is associated with online trading? Well first of all, there are two types of tax that will apply: The first is IT, USC and PRSI – all of which apply to your trading income. Capital gains tax on the other hand applies to the disposal of shares. Taxation on the sale of stocks and shares is non different to that of regular investments or things like property sales. It doesn’t matter how you trade them, you must pay CGT on any gains that you make. Oh and in case you’re wondering, capital gains tax is 33% of your profits. So what else do you need to know? There are factors like regulation of offshore funds for example. Offshore funds and dividend-paying shares are some of the most heavily taxed investment products and can amount to as much as 55% - offshore funds can be subject to income tax as well as a much higher capital gain tax rate. You might also be interested to know that things like accountancy fees can be deducted as an expense from your final tax bill. When it comes to your trading income, you’ll be eligible for a trading deduction for accountancy or legal fees amongst other things as long as they are exclusively for the purpose of trade. When it comes to CGT on the other hand, accountancy fees may be allowable against sales proceeds from the disposal of assets as well as any brokerage fees. You see, knowing what’s what when it comes to filing your taxes can make all the difference. There are lots of things that can making paying tax on online virtual shares confusing and that’s where Taxback.com can help you.
