CFD derives its value from the price movements of the underlying asset, and trading a CFD involves entering into an agreement with a broker to settle the spread (i.e. the difference) between the price of an asset at the opening and at the closure of the contract. Hence you are able to take advantage of this agreement in case the price moves in the direction of your expectation. So for example, if you are expecting a stock to go up in price between the time you open and close position, opening a long position in CFD would allow taking a profit i.e. the difference, in case the expectation you hoped for proves to be right. eToro offers several assets that can be traded as CFD’s on the platform, including stocks, commodities, indices, currencies, and cryptocurrencies, all of which you can monitor on your ‘Watchlist’. The advantages of CFD: One of the main reasons to use a CFD is the leverage it allows for, as you only need a margin to open a position rather than the full value Since you are engaging only a fraction of your capital in an open position, the rest of the capital is borrowed from the broker. So for example, purchasing 100 shares of a company each one costing 50$ (so worth 5 000$ for the position in total) using CFD’s with x5 leverage would require depositing a margin of only 1 000$ rather than the full amount. CFD also allows for a short position to be taken on a financial product, which is not permitted for many assets like stocks or commodities, so there is a possibility for you to profit off declining markets. For example, you anticipate a decrease in share price due to unfavorable news or market conditions and want to take advantage of that by selling contracts now and take the profit when asset price drops in the future. eToro likewise offers various levels of leverage for short-selling transactions on the platform, which you can see after clicking on the chosen asset as shown in the image below.
