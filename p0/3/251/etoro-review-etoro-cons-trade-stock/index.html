But for this video, I’ve really put some thought into some of the aspects that are not that ideal when using eToro, especially for me. So with no further a due, let’s get to the subject of the day. Some cons, when using eToro. 1. Crypto fees When investing into Crypto currencies specifically, eToro is not necessarily the cheapest crypto exchange. Having used eToro for buying bitcoin and other cryptocurrency, I have definitely found that you can buy cryptocurrency a bit cheaper on other platforms. It’s not the worst exchange and I will definitely use eToro again in the future when buying crypto, just because the platform is really nice and the amount I invest in to crypto is a very small percentage of my investing. But if you only invest in to crypto and you buy a whole bunch of crypto more than just BTC, you will find other platforms more suitable for this purpose. And this is probably because eToro is firstly a platform for trading or investing into stocks and not a solely a crypto exchange. 2. Crypto Wallet This point definitely links with the first point I talked about. But from what I have experienced, eToro’s Crypto Wallet is not my favourite. So, if you want to transfer cryptocurrency to your own personal wallet, where you’re crypto is generally safer, after purchasing it on eToro you will need to transfer it from the eToro exchange through to the eToro Crypto wallet first. Now, besides the eToro Crypto wallet taking an extra fee for the transfer, it also takes 2 to 3 days usually for the crypto to show in the eToro wallet and according to eToro’s website it can take up to 5 business days. Which is not necessarily ideal for many of us, especially in the crypto space and if you are more of a trader rather than an investor. If you are invested into crypto for the long run, this might not necessarily be such a big disadvantage to you. 3. Dividends aren’t automatically reinvested within the original investment position that generates the dividend pay-out Okay so why can dividends be a bit of a pain on eToro. Well, when you are invested in to an ETF or stock that pays out dividends a couple times a year or quarterly like most of my ETFs, then the dividends that are paid out will not be automatically reinvested in to the position that paid out the dividends in the first place. Instead, the dividend pay-out amount will be added to your Funds available and you can then decide what to do with that money. 4. When withdrawing money from eToro, it has to exit the platform exactly the same as how it was added So as an example, if you added funds onto eToro through a wire transfer from your bank account, any profits you want to withdraw from your eToro account will be paid back into that same account. You do not have the option to pay those profits out to a PayPal account for instance. And vicar versa of course. 5. Some eToro customers might be limited to certain trading or investment options, depending on the region One reason I like eToro is because I can buy the “Real underlying asset” of stocks or ETFs. But I know that this option is limited to some eToro customers because of their region or country. Instead, these customers will have to be satisfied with only buying the CFD of stocks and ETFs. Luckily, according to what I know this is only the case in a hand full of countries and this can always change in the future. eToro has already offered “The real underlying assets” of stocks to more and more regions lately. I am not too sure on why this is the case, but my guess would be it has to do with certain licensing agreements and so on.
