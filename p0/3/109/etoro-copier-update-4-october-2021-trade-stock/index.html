Let's take a look. Looking back over the past 12 months: S&P500 12-month: +29% wesl3y 12-month: +38% 👀 ᴡᴇᴇᴋ ɪɴ ʀᴇᴠɪᴇᴡ 👑 ᴛʜᴇ ᴜɴɪᴛᴇᴅ ᴋɪɴɢᴅᴏᴍ UK stocks pulled back a little last week but by and large they had a good week. The exception is $ASOS.L which dropped dramatically when competitor Boohoo posted weak results on rising costs. As I've said before, anything with a supply chain is going to have some pain right now but this creates opportunity. I still think ASOS is worth at least double the current share price. Economic data out of the UK showed stronger GDP growth than expected with manufacturing PMI higher than thought. However, our headlines have been dominated by a potential "energy crisis" and gasoline shortage although it already looks as though both have abated. However, supply chain issues and an enormous shortage of truck drivers are likely to continue driving prices higher across the board. On the bright side, the UK's new travel system takes effect today with restrictions easing even further through the month. And whilst coronavirus case numbers in the UK are still elevated, deaths and hospitalisations are falling rapidly 🦅 ᴛʜᴇ ᴜɴɪᴛᴇᴅ sᴛᴀᴛᴇs There have been quite a few headline items in the US recently: the debt ceiling challenge, the stimulus bill, $NSDQ100 and $SPX500 having their worst month since March 2020, higher jobless claims than expected, supply chain issues going into Q4, etc. A decent amount of "heat" has been taken out of US stocks, and the fall in indices doesn't tell the whole picture. A large number of stocks in both indices are in correction territory, and some have been there for longer than a month. The correction in US equities has been going on for longer and has been deeper than the indices tell us. This is because of how much influence the mega-caps have on these index values. We also saw ISM manufacturing PMI data out of the US which at 61 is firmly in growth territory - this is also a leading indicator (tells us what is to come), so despite all the negative sentiment out there we know that growth is going in the right direction. $MU reported fantastic earnings but pointed to temporary weakness in demand because of - you guessed it - supply chain issues. Their longer-term guidance looks great. The stock price dropped a little but was quite resilient and I suspect we'll see it amble northwards over the coming months. 🐉 ᴄʜɪɴᴀ China has interesting news just about every week, and last week was no exception. There have been several measures to cushion the effect a collapse in Evergrande might have including a liquidity injection, and steps to ring-fence the business. But the biggest news of the week was probably the "energy crisis" in China. China's growth combined with moves towards electrification and sustainable energy has resulted in a shortfall that's meant blackouts in several parts of the country. The government has even asked power-hungry industries to dial back their operations which will dent economic growth. China - and a few other countries - are learning that moving from fossil fuel to renewables whilst growing your economy will have all kinds of challenges. Chinese stocks have reacted negatively to this turn of events, which makes sense since their local - and our global - supply chain issues are likely to get worse for the coming months. $KWEB which is an ETF of Chinese technology stocks is back to March 2020 levels and may sit there for some time. Earnings season will be very interesting, but could also be a catalyst for big moves in these stocks. China is now a very obvious opportunity, but no one wants to be the first one to jump in while the risk of more regulation is out there. But once investors do return I expect the floodgates to open and prices to rise fast. 🔮 ᴛʜɪs ᴡᴇᴇᴋ Most of this week (since last Friday) sees a national holiday in China. We'll also see a smattering of economic data out of the UK and US but these are unlikely to make big waves. US earnings reports will start coming in as we approach the middle of the month and I expect that the whales will start accumulating their favourites right about now. Don't forget, we're now in the Shopping Quarter. Singles Day, Black Friday, Cyber Monday, Christmas, Boxing Day, etc are all in this quarter. I also think that people are going to bring some of their shopping forward because of anticipated supply chain issues. This will have some interesting impacts. Have a great week! 🖖 Disclaimer: This is my analysis and opinion, not investment advice. I might own some of the stocks and other assets mentioned, please check my etoro portfolio to find out.
